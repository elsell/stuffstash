Stuff Stash is built around four everyday inventory jobs.

## Ask Where Something Is

Use normal household language:

> Where is the fertilizer?

Stuff Stash searches the inventory you can access and answers in terms you
recognize: garage shelf, office bin, medicine cabinet, wire rack.

<img src="../screenshots/workspace.png" alt="Inventory workspace preview" />

## Add Things Before The Moment Passes

Inventory gets stale when adding one thing takes too long. Stuff Stash keeps add
flows close to the main workspace so you can capture the item, location, and
photo while you still have the thing in your hand.

<img src="../screenshots/add-item.png" alt="Add item preview" />

## Search When You Want To Scan

Voice is fast when you know what you want. Search is better when you want to
compare close matches, inspect similar items, or avoid speaking.

<img src="../screenshots/search.png" alt="Search preview" />

## Review Changes Before They Save

Conversational updates should be fast without being reckless. When a voice or
text command changes inventory state, Stuff Stash shows the planned action before
it saves.

<img src="../screenshots/voice-review.png" alt="Conversational review preview" />

## Built-In Product Promises

| Promise | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| Low-friction upkeep | Voice and text workflows help you maintain the inventory while doing real work. |
| Photos first | Item photos and receipt images help you recognize what you own. |
| SSO-native | Sign in with an OIDC provider instead of local-only accounts. |
| Scoped sharing | Share an inventory without exposing everything else. |
| Portable data | Import and export keep your inventory from being trapped. |
