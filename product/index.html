Product

Stuff Stash is for the household inventory you actually need to keep current: tools in bins, medicine in cabinets, receipts in photos, documents in folders, and the thing you swear you saw last summer.

The product is built around low-friction upkeep. Inventory only works when it is easy to update while you are already doing something else.

The previews below show the product experience Stuff Stash is built around.

The Main Flow

Section titled “The Main Flow”

Open Stuff Stash, start voice mode, and ask a normal question:

Where is the fertilizer?

Stuff Stash should search the inventory you can access, answer in household language, and let you keep going. If you say:

Move it from the garage shelf to the wire rack.

Stuff Stash should show the plan before saving anything.

Fast Add

Section titled “Fast Add”

Add items, containers, locations, and photos from the same workspace. The goal is not to make a perfect catalog in one sitting. The goal is to make the next update small enough that you will actually do it.

Search When You Want Control

Section titled “Search When You Want Control”

Voice is the fastest path for many jobs, but search stays first-class. Search is useful when you want to scan matches, compare similar items, or avoid speaking.

Review Before Save

Section titled “Review Before Save”

Conversational updates are designed to be quick without being reckless. For meaningful changes, Stuff Stash shows what it plans to do before it applies the change.

What Stuff Stash Tracks

Section titled “What Stuff Stash Tracks”

Stuff Stash models real household structure:

  • Items are normal things you want to find, move, document, or share.
  • Containers are things that can hold other things, such as bins or toolboxes.
  • Locations are places, such as a garage, closet, shelf, or cabinet.
  • Photos and files help identify items and preserve receipts or documents.
  • Custom fields and types let one inventory track medicine differently from tools.
  • History records what changed and supports safer undo-oriented workflows.

Data Belongs To You

Section titled “Data Belongs To You”

Import and export are core trust promises. Stuff Stash is designed for JSON and CSV portability, with migration paths from existing self-hosted inventory tools planned around the same import boundary.
